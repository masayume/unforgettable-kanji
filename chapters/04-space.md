**Zen 然 (ぜん) - sort of thing, so, if so, well. - 401 of 2500**

\begin{tabular}{|m{6cm}|m{0.8cm}|m{1.4cm}|m{0.8cm}|m{6cm}|}
\hline
Upper Radicals & & Kanji & & Lower Radical \\
\hline
月 (nikuzuki): from 肉 "flesh". 犬: "dog". & \Huge 月犬 & \scalebox{2}{\Huge 然} & \Huge 灬 & "Fire" or "flames" when placed at the bottom of a kanji. \\
\hline
\end{tabular}

Surviving out in wild **nature** (自然). You have hunted some **meat** (月) and you share it with your loyal **dog** (犬) as you both sit warming yourselves over a **roaring fire** (灬). \hl{This raw, unrefined state of sitting by a fire with a dog and food represents nature and the fundamental state of how things are.}

On\'yomi: ゼン, ネン (ZEN)

- 自然 (しぜん / shizen) - Nature; occurring naturally (without human influence).
- 突然 (とつぜん / totsuzen) - suddenly; 突: stab, thrust, pierce, collision, sudden.
- 全然 (ぜんぜん / zenzen) – entirely, not at all.

Kun\'yomi: しか, しか.り, しか.し, さ

- 然し(しかし / shikashi) - however, but.

**Sakai, Kyou, Kei 境 (キョウ,さかい) - Boundary, border, region, environment - 346 of 2500**

\begin{tabular}{|m{6cm}|m{0.8cm}|m{1.4cm}|m{0.8cm}|m{6cm}|}
\hline
Right Radical & & Kanji & & Left Radical \\
\hline
(tsuchihen) - This radical means "earth," "soil," or "ground.". & \Huge 土 & \scalebox{2}{\Huge 境} & \Huge 竟 & "end" or "finally." Visually, it can be broken down into two distinct parts: 音 (sound/noise) sitting on top of 儿 (human legs/person). \\
\hline
\end{tabular}

Imagine a person standing on their own two **legs (儿)** at the very edge of their land. They stomp the **earth (土)** and project a loud, echoing **sound (音)** across the landscape to declare, "This is where my territory ends!"
\hl{Using sound and the earth to mark where the land ends creates a clear boundary or border.}

On\'yomi: キョウ (KYŌ)

- 環境  (かんきょう / kankyou) – environment; 環: ring, circle, loop.
- 国境 (こっきょう / kokkyō) – national border.
- 境界 (きょうかい / kyōkai) – boundary, limit.

Kun\'yomi: さかい (sakai)

- 境 (さかい / sakai) – border, boundary (often used for physical property lines or the border between two states of being, like life and death).
