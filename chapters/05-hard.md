**Kan 関 (せき、-ぜき、かか.わる、からくり、かんぬき) - connection, relation, involvement. 70 of 2500**

\begin{tabular}{|m{6cm}|m{0.8cm}|m{1.4cm}|m{0.8cm}|m{6cm}|}
\hline
Upper Radicals & & Kanji & & Lower Radical \\
\hline
(mongamae) - The "gate" radical. It frames the outside of the kanji and is used in characters related to doors, entrances, enclosures, and spaces between things. & \Huge 門 & \scalebox{2}{\Huge 関} & \Huge 关 & frontier pass, close, relation \\
\hline
\end{tabular}

Imagine a massive set of gates (門) that reach all the way up to heaven (天), with two warning lights (丷) glowing at the very top. This heavenly gate acts as a strict barrier or checkpoint, controlling who is allowed to enter and managing the connection between two worlds.


On\'yomi: カン (KAN)

- 関係 (かんけい / kankei) – relationship, connection. 係 (ケイ) - person in charge, connection, duty, concern.
- 関心 (かんしん / kanshin) – interest, concern.

Kun\'yomi: せき (seki), かか-わる (kaka.waru)

- 関 (せき / seki) – checkpoint, barrier.
- 関わる (かかわる / kakawaru) – to be involved in, to have to do with.

**Dai 題 (ダイ) - Topic, subject, theme, title.** 

\begin{tabular}{|m{6cm}|m{0.8cm}|m{1.4cm}|m{0.8cm}|m{6cm}|}
\hline
Upper Radicals & & Kanji & & Lower Radical \\
\hline
This kanji means "correct," "right," or "just." & \Huge 是 & \scalebox{2}{\Huge 題} & \Huge 頁 & (ōgai) - This radical means "head" or "page." It is commonly found in kanji related to the head, face, or documents (like 顔 - face, or 頭 - head). \\
\hline
\end{tabular}

\hl{Imagine you are sitting down to do your homework. You look at the page (頁) in front of you, using your head (頁) to find the correct (是) answer to the problem or topic you were given.}
Putting the correct idea on the page is how you answer the theme or problem.

On\'yomi: ダイ (DAI)

- 宿題 (しゅくだい / shukudai) – homework. 宿 (やど) - home, dwelling, hotel.
- 問題 (もんだい / mondai) – problem, question.
- 話題 (わだい / wadai) – topic of conversation.

Kun\'yomi: none in everyday use
