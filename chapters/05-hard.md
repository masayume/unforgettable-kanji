# Jyu, Sazu 授 (ジュ,さず) - To impart, to instruct, to grant, to confer.

| Radical | | Kanji | | Right Part |
| :----------------------------------------------------------- | :--- | :-----: | ---: | -----------------------------------------------------------: |
| (tehen) - The "hand" radical, used for actions involving the hands. |  \vspace{1.1em} \Large 扌 | \vspace{0.8em} \Huge 授 | \vspace{1.1em} \Large 受 | This kanji on its own means "to receive" or "to accept." small. | 
Imagine extending your hand to give someone a gift or knowledge. \hl{A hand (扌) is reaching out so that the other person can receive (受).}
The act of using your hand to help someone receive something means you are imparting or granting it to them.

On\'yomi: ジュ (JU)

- 授業 (じゅぎょう / jugyō) – class, lesson. 業 (ぎょう) - business, vocation, arts, performance.
- 教授 (きょうじゅ / kyōju) – professor (literally "teaching and imparting").

Kun\'yomi: さず-ける (sazu.keru), さず-かる (sazu.karu)

- 授ける (さずける / sazukeru) – to grant, to award, to impart (transitive).
- 授かる (さずかる / sazukaru) – to be awarded, to be gifted with (intransitive).

# Kyou, Oshieru 教 (きょう, おし.える) - Teach, faith, doctrine.

| Radical | | Kanji | | Right Part |
| :----------------------------------------------------------- | :--- | :-----: | ---: | -----------------------------------------------------------: |
| This means "filial piety" (respect for parents and ancestors). If you break it down further, it is composed of an elder/old person (耂) looking over a child (子). | \vspace{1.1em} \Large 孝 | \vspace{0.8em} \Huge 教 | \vspace{1.1em} \Large 攵 | (bokuzukuri or nobunome) — This radical means "to strike" or "action." It often depicts a hand holding a stick or tool. | 
On the left, you have an elder (耂) passing down wisdom and good behavior to a child (子), representing filial piety (孝). On the right, the teacher holds a stick (攵) to tap the chalkboard or enforce discipline.
\hl{The act of an elder guiding a child with strict action (or a pointer stick) is the universal symbol for teaching.}

On\'yomi: キョウ (KYŌ)

- 教室 (きょうしつ / kyōshitsu) – classroom. 室 (しつ) - room.
- 教育 (きょういく / kyōiku) – education. 育 (そだ, イク) - bring up, grow up, raise, rear.
- 宗教 (しゅうきょう / shūkyō) - religion. 宗 (しゅう) - religion, sect, denomination, main point, origin, essence.

Kun\'yomi: おし-える (oshi.eru), おそ-わる (oso.waru)

- 教える (おしえる / oshieru) – to teach, to inform.
- 教わる (おそわる / osowaru) – to be taught, to learn from someone.

# Dai 題 (ダイ) - Topic, subject, theme, title. 

| Radical | | Kanji | | Right Part |
| :----------------------------------------------------------- | :--- | :-----: | ---: | -----------------------------------------------------------: |
| This kanji means "correct," "right," or "just." | \vspace{1.1em} \Large 是 | \vspace{0.8em} \Huge 題 | \vspace{1.1em} \Large 頁 | (ōgai) — This radical means "head" or "page." It is commonly found in kanji related to the head, face, or documents (like 顔 - face, or 頭 - head). | 

\hl{Imagine you are sitting down to do your homework. You look at the page (頁) in front of you, using your head (頁) to find the correct (是) answer to the problem or topic you were given.}
Putting the correct idea on the page is how you answer the theme or problem.

On\'yomi: ダイ (DAI)

- 宿題 (しゅくだい / shukudai) – homework. 宿 (やど) - home, dwelling, hotel.
- 問題 (もんだい / mondai) – problem, question.
- 話題 (わだい / wadai) – topic of conversation.

Kun\'yomi: none in everyday use
