**Kyou, mune 胸 (キョウ; むね　むな) - bosom, breast, chest, heart. 1144 of 2500**

\begin{tabular}{|m{6cm}|m{0.8cm}|m{1.4cm}|m{0.8cm}|m{6cm}|}
\hline
Upper Radicals & & Kanji & & Lower Radical \\
\hline
月 (nikuzuki): from 肉 "flesh". It is the standard radical for body parts. & \Huge 月 & \scalebox{2}{\Huge 胸} & \Huge 匈 & 勹 (tsutsumigamae), meaning "to wrap" or "to enclose.". 凶 (kyō), meaning "bad," "evil," or "misfortune." \\
\hline
\end{tabular}

Your chest is the sturdy part of your **flesh (月)** where your ribs wrap around and **enclose (勹)** your vital organs to protect your heart from fatal or **bad (凶)** blows.
\hl{Imagine your emotions are trapped in your chest/your flesh (月) securely wraps (勹) around your heart, keeping out the bad (凶) things in the world.}

On'yomi (Chinese reading): キョウ (KYŌ)

- 胸囲 (きょうい / kyōi) – chest measurement, girth. 囲 (i): surround, besiege, store, enclosure, preserve, keep.
- 胸中 (きょうちゅう / kyōchū) – in one's heart, one's true feelings.

Kun'yomi (Japanese reading): むね (mune), むな- (muna-)

- 胸 (むね / mune) – chest, breast.
- 胸騒ぎ (むなさわぎ / munasawagi) – uneasiness, premonition (literally "noisy chest").
- 胸元 (むなもと / munamoto) – the breast (of a coat/shirt), near the chest.

**Ude, WAN - 腕 (キョウ; むね　むな) - arm - 0000 of 2500**

\begin{tabular}{|m{6cm}|m{0.8cm}|m{1.4cm}|m{0.8cm}|m{6cm}|}
\hline
Upper Radicals & & Kanji & & Lower Radical \\
\hline
月 (nikuzuki): from 肉 "flesh". It is the standard radical for body parts. & \Huge 月 & \scalebox{2}{\Huge 腕} & \Huge 宛 & "address" or "to allocate." 3 pieces: 宀 (ukanmuri): A roof; 夕 (yū): Evening; 卩 (fushizukuri): A kneeling person (or a seal). \\
\hline
\end{tabular}

Imagine your physical **body/flesh (月)** working hard under a **roof (宀)** late into the **evening (夕)**. You are a **kneeling person (卩)** using all the strength in your arm to finish writing a large stack of **addresses (宛)**.
\hl{The body part (flesh) you use to tirelessly write out an address under a roof at night is your arm.}

On'yomi (Chinese reading): ワン (WAN)

- 腕力 (わんりょく / wanryoku) – physical strength, brute force.
- 腕白 (わんぱく / wanpaku) – naughty, mischievous (usually describing a highly energetic child).

Kun'yomi (Japanese reading): うで (ude)

- 腕 (うで / ude) – arm (also used to mean "skill").
- 腕時計 (うでどけい / udedokei) – wristwatch.
- 腕前 (うでまえ / udemae) – ability, skill, dexterity.
